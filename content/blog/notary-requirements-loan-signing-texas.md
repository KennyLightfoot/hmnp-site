# Notary Requirements for Loan Signing in Texas: Complete Guide

Loan signings require proper notarization to ensure documents are legally valid and enforceable. Whether you're closing on a home purchase, refinancing, or getting a HELOC, understanding notary requirements is crucial. This guide covers everything you need to know about notarizing loan documents in Texas.

## Why Loan Documents Must Be Notarized

### Legal Requirements:
- **Mortgage documents**: Must be notarized to be recorded
- **Deed of trust**: Requires notarization for validity
- **Promissory notes**: Often require notarization
- **Legal enforceability**: Notarized documents are legally binding

### Lender Requirements:
- **Bank policies**: Most lenders require notarization
- **Title companies**: Require notarization for title insurance
- **Government agencies**: FHA, VA, USDA loans require notarization
- **Secondary market**: Required for loan sales to investors

## Notary Requirements for Loan Signings

### What You Need:
- **Valid photo ID**: Driver's license, passport, or state ID
- **Loan documents**: Provided by lender or title company
- **All signers present**: All parties must be present
- **Payment**: For notary fees and closing costs
- **Reading time**: Time to review documents

### What the Notary Will Do:
1. Verify identity of all signers
2. Ensure documents are complete
3. Witness all signatures
4. Notarize required documents
5. Apply notary seal and signature
6. Return documents to lender/title company

## Required Documents for Loan Signings

### Standard Documents:
- **Note**: Promissory note (loan amount, terms)
- **Deed of Trust**: Security instrument
- **Closing Disclosure**: Final loan terms
- **Affidavits**: Various sworn statements
- **Disclosures**: Required lender disclosures

### Additional Documents (varies by loan type):
- **FHA/VA addendums**: Government loan requirements
- **Flood insurance**: If in flood zone
- **Homeowners insurance**: Proof of coverage
- **Title documents**: Title insurance paperwork

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do all loan documents need to be notarized?
No, only certain documents require notarization (typically the mortgage/deed of trust and promissory note). Other documents may be signed without notarization.

### Can I use Remote Online Notarization for loan signings?
Yes, RON is available in Texas for loan signings, but some lenders and title companies may prefer in-person notarization.

### How long does a loan signing take?
Typically 30-45 minutes for most loan signings, depending on loan type and document complexity.

### What if I can't attend the signing?
All required signers must be present. If you can't attend, you may need to reschedule or use a power of attorney (which also requires notarization).

### Can I sign loan documents before the notary arrives?
No, documents must be signed in the notary's presence. Signing beforehand invalidates the notarization.

### What happens if my ID is expired?
You'll need to renew your ID or use an alternative valid ID. Expired ID cannot be accepted.
