**Quick Answer:** Notarization costs in Houston range from $50-$150 for mobile service, depending on your specific needs. Here's exactly how much you can expect to pay and what factors affect the cost.

If you've never hired a notary before, pricing can seem confusing. Between state-regulated fees, service charges, and travel costs, it's hard to know what you should actually pay. This guide breaks down every cost component so you can budget accurately and avoid surprises.

## Understanding Notary Pricing: The Two-Part Structure

### **Part 1: State-Regulated Notary Fees**
Texas law sets maximum fees that notaries can charge for official notarial acts:
- **Acknowledgments:** $10 per signature
- **Jurats:** $10 per signature
- **Oaths/Affirmations:** $10 per signature
- **Certified Copies:** $10 per document

### **Part 2: Service Fees**
Mobile notary services charge additional fees for:
- **Travel time and expenses**
- **Professional expertise**
- **Equipment and supplies**
- **Scheduling flexibility**
- **Insurance and liability**
