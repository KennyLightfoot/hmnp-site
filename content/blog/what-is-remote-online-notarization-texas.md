# What is Remote Online Notarization (RON) in Texas? Complete Guide 2024

Remote Online Notarization (RON) is a modern way to have documents notarized via video conference, eliminating the need for physical presence with a notary. Texas became one of the first states to allow RON in 2017, making it easier and more convenient to get documents notarized. This guide explains everything you need to know about RON in Texas.

## What is Remote Online Notarization (RON)?

Remote Online Notarization allows you to have documents notarized via secure video conference with a certified RON notary. Instead of meeting in person, you connect with a notary through a secure online platform, verify your identity electronically, and sign documents digitally.

### Key Features:
- **Video conference**: Connect with notary via secure video call
- **Electronic signing**: Sign documents digitally
- **Identity verification**: Verify identity using technology
- **Secure platform**: Encrypted, secure transmission
- **Recorded sessions**: Sessions are recorded and stored

## How Does RON Work?

### Step 1: Schedule Appointment
- Choose RON platform or service
- Schedule appointment time
- Receive meeting link and instructions

### Step 2: Prepare Documents
- Upload documents to platform
- Ensure documents are complete
- Review documents before appointment

### Step 3: Connect with Notary
- Join video call at scheduled time
- Verify your identity using:
  - Knowledge-based authentication (KBA) questions
  - Credential analysis (ID scanning)
  - Biometric verification (some platforms)

### Step 4: Review Documents
- Notary reviews documents with you
- Discuss any questions or concerns
- Ensure you understand what you're signing

### Step 5: Sign Documents
- Sign documents electronically
- Notary witnesses signature via video
- Notary applies electronic seal and signature

### Step 6: Receive Documents
- Receive notarized documents electronically
- Download PDF copies
- Original documents stored securely

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is RON legal in Texas?
Yes, RON has been legal in Texas since 2017 and is recognized for most documents.

### Are RON documents accepted by banks and title companies?
Most institutions accept RON documents, but some may prefer in-person notarization for high-value transactions. Check with your institution first.

### Can I use RON for real estate closings?
Yes, RON is legal for real estate closings in Texas, but some title companies may prefer in-person notarization. Confirm with your title company.

### Is RON more secure than in-person?
Both methods are secure when done properly. RON has digital security features, while in-person has physical security. Choose based on your comfort level.

### Can I use RON if I'm not tech-savvy?
RON requires basic technology skills. If you're uncomfortable with video calls or technology, in-person may be better.

### How much does RON cost compared to in-person?
RON typically costs $25-$50, while in-person ranges from free (bank) to $150+ (mobile notary). RON is often more cost-effective than mobile notary services.

### What if I don't have reliable internet?
RON requires reliable internet. If you don't have good connectivity, in-person notarization may be better.
