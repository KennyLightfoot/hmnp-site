# How to Get Documents Notarized for Real Estate in Texas: Complete Guide

Real estate transactions require proper notarization to ensure documents are legally valid and can be recorded with county clerks. Whether you're buying, selling, or refinancing property, understanding real estate notarization requirements is essential. This guide covers everything you need to know about notarizing real estate documents in Texas.

## Why Real Estate Documents Must Be Notarized

### Legal Requirements:
- **Deed recording**: Deeds must be notarized to be recorded
- **Legal validity**: Notarized documents are legally binding
- **Title insurance**: Required for title insurance policies
- **Court recognition**: Notarized documents are admissible in court

### Practical Benefits:
- **Prevents fraud**: Verifies identity of signers
- **Ensures validity**: Documents are legally enforceable
- **Facilitates recording**: County clerks require notarization
- **Protects parties**: All parties are protected legally

## Real Estate Documents That Require Notarization

### 1. Deeds
- **Warranty deed**: Transfers property with warranty
- **Quitclaim deed**: Transfers property without warranty
- **Deed of trust**: Security instrument for mortgage
- **Transfer on death deed**: Transfers property upon death

### 2. Mortgage Documents
- **Mortgage note**: Promissory note for loan
- **Deed of trust**: Security instrument
- **Mortgage modification**: Changes to mortgage terms
- **Satisfaction of mortgage**: Release of mortgage lien

### 3. Real Estate Contracts
- **Purchase agreement**: Contract to buy property
- **Sale agreement**: Contract to sell property
- **Lease agreements**: Commercial leases (some)
- **Option agreements**: Property option contracts

### 4. Title Documents
- **Title affidavits**: Sworn statements about title
- **Title transfer documents**: Transfer of title
- **Title insurance documents**: Title insurance paperwork

### 5. Closing Documents
- **Closing statements**: Final settlement statements
- **Affidavits**: Various sworn statements
- **Disclosures**: Required property disclosures

## Requirements for Real Estate Notarization

### What You Need:
- **Valid photo ID**: Driver's license, passport, or state ID
- **Real estate documents**: Provided by title company or attorney
- **All signers present**: All parties must be present
- **Payment**: For notary fees and closing costs
- **Property information**: Property address, legal description

### What the Notary Will Do:
1. Verify identity of all signers
2. Ensure documents are complete
3. Witness all signatures
4. Notarize required documents
5. Apply notary seal and signature
6. Return documents to title company/attorney

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Receive Documents
- Title company/attorney sends documents
- Review documents before signing appointment
- Note any questions or concerns
- Prepare list of questions for notary

### Step 2: Schedule Notary Appointment
- Coordinate with title company/attorney
- Schedule convenient time and location
- Ensure all signers can attend
- Confirm notary availability

### Step 3: Prepare for Appointment
- Gather valid photo ID for all signers
- Review documents thoroughly
- Prepare questions
- Arrange payment method

### Step 4: Meet with Notary
- Present valid ID
- Review documents together
- Ask questions
- Sign documents in notary's presence

### Step 5: Notarization
- Notary verifies identity
- Notary witnesses signatures
- Notary applies seal and signature
- Notary records in journal

### Step 6: Return Documents
- Notary returns documents to title company/attorney
- Documents recorded with county clerk
- Transaction completes
- Title transfers

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do all real estate documents need to be notarized?
No, only certain documents require notarization (typically deeds and mortgages). Other documents may be signed without notarization.

### Can I use Remote Online Notarization for real estate?
Yes, RON is available in Texas for real estate documents, but some title companies may prefer in-person notarization.

### How long does real estate notarization take?
Typically 30-60 minutes depending on the number of documents and signers.

### What if I can't attend the signing?
All required signers must be present. If you can't attend, you may need to reschedule or use a power of attorney (which also requires notarization).

### Can I sign real estate documents before the notary arrives?
No, documents must be signed in the notary's presence. Signing beforehand invalidates the notarization.

### What happens if my ID is expired?
You'll need to renew your ID or use an alternative valid ID. Expired ID cannot be accepted.
